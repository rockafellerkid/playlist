  • Breaking in Brooklyn

    Dogpark

    Found this a few weeks ago, thought it'd be a good one to start off with. Pretty upbeat, nice beat, good vibes!

  • Cali's Song

    Runner and Bobby

    This was one of those songs that I randomly stumbled across while doing labwork and the guitar riff came on and I thought "ohhhh my god"

  • Freeway

    Night Cap

    This is another pretty upbeat jam, liked the brightness of the vocals a lot!

  • Delay

    Rec Hall

    I really thought this song sounded like "Alive" by Empire Of The Sun. Can't put my finger on it but it sounds really fun regardless!

  • supertight

    georgee

    This song kinda had a funkier sound to it that I really enjoyed. The drums really drive this song, with the occasional overuse of the cymbals at some times but still good nonetheless.

  • Life On The Line

    The Walters

    Shortest song that I'm sending. Quick little beat to start the vibes of the playlist towards the sad direction. An upbeat tempo with sadder lyrics never hurt anyone...right?

  • Enjoy Responsibly

    Runner and Bobby

    Another Runner and Bobby song, although not with a sick ass guitar solo at the end. This is a more monotone pick but I still like the way that it sounds and although the guitar isn't carrying as much, the drums sure are.

  • Girlfirend

    Alix Page

    The harmonizing is fantastic, chorus is killer, lyrics exploring the beginnings of a heavy crush, good vibes all around with lots of good emotion in the singing.

  • Pretty Girl

    Zachary Leo

    I heard this and immediatly fucked with the guitar riff. Reminds me of Malcom Todd's stuff, especially earlier on in his career (which isn't that deep at all yet anyway). Some of the lyrics don't make much sense, but that doesn't matter if it sounds good!

  • Always on My Mind

    Roller Derby

    Aside from me seeing the band name and going "hey, cool. roller derby", this song got my attention pretty early last week with the last beginning of the chorus where all the music stops for a brief moment for emphasis. Creates a really fun end to the song.

  • Flash In The Pan

    Wallice

    The brass helps this song stand out among the rest of the ones in this playlist. It'll get stuck in your head and keep you coming back to replay it over and over.

  • The Trip

    Still Corners

    I really loved how this song starts. The silly little 4 note walk-down was captivating and then the smooth singing over the music helped keep my attention in check. Later in the song when the guitar comes in is just wonderful.
